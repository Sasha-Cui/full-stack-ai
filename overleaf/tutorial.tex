\begin{abstract}
Modern AI research increasingly requires fluency across the full technical stack: data curation, training frameworks, scaling laws, distributed systems, parameter-efficient adaptation, post-training, evaluation, inference, and agentic workflows. This tutorial accompanies the Yale ``Becoming Full-Stack AI Researchers'' working group and organizes those components into an end-to-end roadmap for researchers who want to move beyond isolated model experiments toward robust AI systems. The paper is paired with a public repository of minimal working examples so that readers can reproduce the core workflows locally, understand the systems tradeoffs involved, and extend the material to their own projects.
\end{abstract}
